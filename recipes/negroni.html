The Negroni

The Ingredients:

  • 1 measure, Campari
  • 1 measure, Italian Vermouth, such as Carpano Antico or Carpano Punt e mes
  • 1 measure, London Dry Gin; my favorite at the moment is Ford's
  • Ice, for mixing
  • 1 Large square ice cube, for the drink
  • Orange peel, for garnish

Some equipment you'll need:

  • 1 Cocktail Mixing Glass
  • 1 Cocktail Mixing Spoon
  • Double Old Fashioned Glasses
  • Hawthorne, or Julip, Strainer
  • Vegetable Peeler
  • Ice Cube Molds

To Make the Drink:

  • Using a jigger, measure out equal parts - to your liking - of Campari, Vermouth, and Gin, pouring each into the mixing glass.
  • place ice in the mixing glass and least 3/4 the way up.
  • With the mixing spoon, stir in a circular motion around the edge of the glass, until the ice has begun to melt and it is easier to stir, approximately 15-30 seconds. You want slight dilution of the drink here.
  • Place one large square cube of ice in you double old fashioned - aka rock's - glass, and using the Hawthorne strainer over the mixing glass, pour the drink into the rock's glass, over the ice.
  • Using the vegetable peeler, remove a sliver of orange skin. Holding the peel over the drink, pinch the peel length wise in half - outer skin pointed at the top of the drink - to express the natural orange oil from the peel. Rub the peel on the rim of the glass and place the peel inside the drink to garnish.
  • Congratulations, your Negroni is ready to enjoy.
