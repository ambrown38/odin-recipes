Steak Diane

Ingredients:

  • 2 filet mignon steaks
  • 1 shallot, finely chopped
  • 1 pint, button mushrooms
  • 1 tablespoon, dijon mustard
  • 1/3 cup brandy
  • 2 cups, beef stock
  • heavy cream
  • Kosher salt, to taste

To Make:

Assuming you've dry brined your meat and it's tempered, on a cutting board, place on sheet of plastic wrap, your steak, and then a second sheet of plastic wrap on top, so you've made a plastic wrap steak sandwich. With the flat side of meat mallet, beat you meat until it is one inch thick. Do not season with salt again

Bring you pan to temperature in the method discussed in the Steak au Poivrerecipe.

Cook your steaks to desired doneness, no warmer than medium you savages. Place the steaks somewhere out of the pan to rest.

In the same pan you used to cook the steaks, after removing excess fat, add a touch more oil and then the diced shallot. After 30 seconds add a pint of thinly sliced button mushrooms, white or cremini.

Meanwhile, prep the 1/3 cup of brandy. When mushrooms are cooked sufficiently, remove the pan from the flame and add the brandy - not from the bottle from a seperate mixing container, flambe, and deglaze, scraping the fond from the bottom of the pan. When the brandy is almost gone, add beef stock.

After the beef stock has appropriately reduce, add a spoonful of dijon mustard, mix, and then add heavy cream until the color is as you like it.

Place steaks in the sauce, baste them with the sauce, and then plate the steaks, spooning sauce over the meat.

Enjoy.
