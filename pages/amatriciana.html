Pasta Amatriciana

To Make:

Traditionally, Amatriciana is made with guanciale and bucatini. The guanciale is from Amatrice, the birthpalce of the pasta. Most of the time I'm not a purest, because Guanciale from Amatrice can be difficult to source here in America. So I'll substitute with pancetta or bacon. You will also need a can of whole tomatoes and Pecorino Romano cheese.

Dump the pasta into a pot of salted, boiling water that tastes like the sea.

Dice the Guanciale, bacon, or pancetta into small cubes. Dump into a cold pan, turn the heat on to medium-low, medium, gradually bringing the heat into the pan. Render out the fat.

Once rendered, remove the meat from the pan and the oil. At this point, you may deglase with a little bit of white wine. Pour the tomatos into the pan, crushing each tomato with your bare hands. Cook over medium to medium high heat.

When the pasta is al dente, remove from water with a pair of kitchen tweezers, and place into the pan with the sauce. Add pasta water as needed in order to cream the sauce.

Once the sauce is creamed, turn off the heat

At this point add in the grated Pecorino Romano, and most of the Guanciale. Sprinkle remaining Guanciale on top as a garnish. Sprinkle cheese as necessary.
